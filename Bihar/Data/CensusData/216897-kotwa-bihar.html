Kotwa Population - Pashchim Champaran, Bihar

Overview

Kotwa is small village located in Bagaha Block of Pashchim Champaran district, Bihar with total 88 families residing. The Kotwa village has population of 477 of which 235 are males while 242 are females as per Population Census 2011.

In Kotwa village population of children with age 0-6 is 91 which makes up 19.08 % of total population of village. Average Sex Ratio of Kotwa village is 1030 which is higher than Bihar state average of 918. Child Sex Ratio for the Kotwa as per census is 1022, higher than Bihar average of 935.

Kotwa village has lower literacy rate compared to Bihar. In 2011, literacy rate of Kotwa village was 55.18 % compared to 61.80 % of Bihar. In Kotwa Male literacy stands at 63.68 % while female literacy rate was 46.94 %.

As per constitution of India and Panchyati Raaj Act, Kotwa village is administrated by Sarpanch (Head of Village) who is elected representative of village.

Caste Factor

In Kotwa village, most of the villagers are from Schedule Caste (SC). Schedule Caste (SC) constitutes 47.17 % of total population in Kotwa village. The village Kotwa currently doesn’t have any Schedule Tribe (ST) population.

Work Profile

In Kotwa village out of total population, 228 were engaged in work activities. 17.98 % of workers describe their work as Main Work (Employment or Earning more than 6 Months) while 82.02 % were involved in Marginal activity providing livelihood for less than 6 months. Of 228 workers engaged in Main Work, 22 were cultivators (owner or co-owner) while 8 were Agricultural labourer.
