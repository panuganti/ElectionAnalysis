Arwal District : Census 2011 data

Arwal District Overview

An official Census 2011 detail of Arwal, a district of Bihar has been released by Directorate of Census Operations in Bihar. Enumeration of key persons was also done by census officials in Arwal District of Bihar.

Arwal District Population 2011

In 2011, Arwal had population of 700,843 of which male and female were 363,497 and 337,346 respectively. In 2001 census, Arwal had a population of 589,476 of which males were 304,645 and remaining 284,831 were females. Arwal District population constituted 0.67 percent of total Maharashtra population. In 2001 census, this figure for Arwal District was at 0.71 percent of Maharashtra population.

Arwal District Population Growth Rate

There was change of 18.89 percent in the population compared to population as per 2001. In the previous census of India 2001, Arwal District recorded increase of 29.84 percent to its population compared to 1991.

Arwal District Density 2011

The initial provisional data released by census India 2011, shows that density of Arwal district for 2011 is 1,098 people per sq. km. In 2001, Arwal district density was at 925 people per sq. km. Arwal district administers 638 square kilometers of areas.

Arwal Literacy Rate 2011

Average literacy rate of Arwal in 2011 were 67.43 compared to 55.31 of 2001. If things are looked out at gender wise, male and female literacy were 79.06 and 54.85 respectively. For 2001 census, same figures stood at 70.95 and 38.66 in Arwal District. Total literate in Arwal District were 386,351 of which male and female were 235,279 and 151,072 respectively. In 2001, Arwal District had 262,605 in its district.

Arwal Sex Ratio 2011

With regards to Sex Ratio in Arwal, it stood at 928 per 1000 male compared to 2001 census figure of 935. The average national sex ratio in India is 940 as per latest reports of Census 2011 Directorate. In 2011 census, child sex ratio is 940 girls per 1000 boys compared to figure of 920 girls per 1000 boys of 2001 census data.

Arwal Child Population 2011

In census enumeration, data regarding child under 0-6 age were also collected for all districts including Arwal. There were total 127,841 children under age of 0-6 against 114,653 of 2001 census. Of total 127,841 male and female were 65,910 and 61,931 respectively. Child Sex Ratio as per census 2011 was 940 compared to 920 of census 2001. In 2011, Children under 0-6 formed 18.24 percent of Arwal District compared to 19.45 percent of 2001. There was net change of -1.21 percent in this compared to previous census of India.

Arwal District Urban Population 2011

Out of the total Arwal population for 2011 census, 7.40 percent lives in urban regions of district. In total 51,849 people lives in urban areas of which males are 27,077 and females are 24,772. Sex Ratio in urban region of Arwal district is 915 as per 2011 census data. Similarly child sex ratio in Arwal district was 934 in 2011 census. Child population (0-6) in urban region was 9,194 of which males and females were 4,754 and 4,440. This child population figure of Arwal district is 17.56 % of total urban population. Average literacy rate in Arwal district as per census 2011 is 75.29 % of which males and females are 85.17 % and 64.45 % literates respectively. In actual number 32,116 people are literate in urban region of which males and females are 19,012 and 13,104 respectively.

Arwal District Rural Population 2011

As per 2011 census, 92.60 % population of Arwal districts lives in rural areas of villages. The total Arwal district population living in rural areas is 648,994 of which males and females are 336,420 and 312,574 respectively. In rural areas of Arwal district, sex ratio is 929 females per 1000 males. If child sex ratio data of Arwal district is considered, figure is 940 girls per 1000 boys. Child population in the age 0-6 is 118,647 in rural areas of which males were 61,156 and females were 57,491. The child population comprises 18.18 % of total rural population of Arwal district. Literacy rate in rural areas of Arwal district is 66.79 % as per census data 2011. Gender wise, male and female literacy stood at 78.57 and 54.09 percent respectively. In total, 354,235 people were literate of which males and females were 216,267 and 137,968 respectively.
